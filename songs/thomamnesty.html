Thom on Channel Four News, 4th December 1998

"I can't really remember how I got involved. It's by default really. You couldn't really help it if you're stuck on a plane all the time and you're going to these places, staying in posh hotels and you're not really seeing anything. After a while you kind of think that there must be something going on, that this is wrong. People have decided that they haven't got any power and that's the end of it."

"The strength of what Amnesty does and the strength of the Declaration of Human Rights is it actually empowers people to know what their rights are. Has it worked? No. Obviously it's not worked yet. It's something to aspire to."

"? is a Buddhist nun who believes that Tibet should be independent from China. She has been sentenced to eighteen years imprisonment by the Chinese authorities for shouting slogans such as Free Tibet, singing pro independence songs and participating in peaceful demonstrations. There is now serious concern for her health."

"Should I use celebrity to highlight issues such as human rights? Well, I don't really use it for anything else cos I... don't. I'm not very comfortable with it [laughs]. What I find really offensive is the way our culture - once anyone has any degree of success - gets into the realms of Hello magazine - 'Have a look at our glamorous lifestyle that you should all be aspiring to - come look the homes of the rich 'n' famous and look at them doing charlie in the toilets. This is your future. This is what you should be looking for, you know. This is success. This is what we're all aiming at.' And I find it f**king obscene, frankly."

"Radiohead came out of the grunge culture of complaint - the X Generation as it was known for a while. But I think that they have grown up - we've all grown up and it's dawned on us that our problems are utterly, utterly irrelevant and it's utterly offensive to have them hammered down your throat on MTV. I feel totally responsible for that."

"The Amnesty gig is a way of trying to redress my guilt, I guess."

"I feel lucky that I'm allowed to speak my mind and write what I like because I'm conscious of the fact that 99% of people are not able to do that. An awful lot of people will get shot or tortured simply by doing just that, in a lot of other countries around the world."
