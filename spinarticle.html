Sound Files by Douglas Wolk

MP3's and other bytes worth your memory

   God bless the trainspotters- the brave fans who willingly sacrifice their own mental well-being to their band of choice, sifting through every crappy live tape and overpriced import remix for the benefit of the less devoted. Radiohead's new album is delayed until sometime next year, but if you can't wait for a fix, creep on over to the Planet Telex site (www.underworld.net/radiohead). It's got 92 RealAudio files representing nearly every song Thom Yorke and crew have ever recorded, and many they haven't: rarities and B-sides, live and acoustic versions of album tracks (like an extended, roughed-up studio take of "Talk Show Host"), a few complete concert sets, and some early demos from the days when they were called On a Friday and wanted badly to be U2. Included is the unreleased live favorite "Man -O- War" as well as "Motion Picture Soundtrack" (a slow dance about suicide) and "How to Disappear Completely and Never Be Found." Both are among the most harrowing pieces they've ever written; Yorke introduces the latter as being "for the benifit of the bootleggers," then rides its curling, gracially slow melody toward the whirpool at its center, moaning, "I'm not here / This isn't happening." Bonus: a text file to make your Mac do the robot speech from "Fitter Happier." (N.B. Radiohead's own site, at www.radiohead.com, is beautiful and arty but has no music at all on it- it's basically an extension of Ok Computer's packaging; Planet Telex may be unofficial, but its vaults probably rival Yorke's.)
